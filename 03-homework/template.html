After the Supreme Court ruled in June that the federal courts lack jurisdiction to decide on partisan gerrymandering, the concern now is how to resolve the bickering over redistricting which gives a party controlling a state undue advantage.

Over the years, both Democratic and Republican parties have engaged in gerrymandering, a practice in which state legislators redraw political districts often to win more congressional seats.

In Maryland where the Democrats have clear dominance in seven out of the eight Congressional districts, redrawing the districts without partisan gerrymandering will reduce their dominance to just three districts, leaving the remaining five to be highly competitive for both parties.
